4 Glossary

AD9833

A programmable waveform generator integrated circuit capable of producing sine, triangular, and square waves

BNC Connector

Bayonet Neill-Concelman connector; a type of RF connector commonly used for signal output in test equipment

ESP32

A series of low-cost, low-power microcontroller with integrated Wi-Fi and Bluetooth capabilities

LCD

Liquid Crystal Display; a flat-panel display technology

OLED

Organic Light-Emitting Diode; a display technology offering high contrast and low power consumption

PCB

Printed Circuit Board; a board that mechanically supports and electrically connects electronic components

RF

Radio Frequency; refers to oscillating electromagnetic waves

SNR

Signal-to-Noise Ratio; measure of signal strength relative to background noise

SPI

Serial Peripheral Interface; a synchronous serial communication protocol

THD

Total Harmonic Distortion; measurement of waveform distortion, expressed as a percentage

USB Type-C

Universal Serial Bus Type-C; a standard connector for power delivery and data transfer

6 Abstract

This project introduces a function generator designed from scratch to produce precise periodic waveforms, including sinusoidal, square, and triangular signals, with adjustable frequency and amplitude. The device is designed for use in academic laboratories, research environments, and small-scale industrial applications, offering a cost-effective and reliable alternative to commercial solutions.

Developed by a team of 60 undergraduate students, this project combines advanced electronic design principles, signal processing expertise, and practical functionality. The function generator integrates oscillators, waveform shaping circuits, and signal conditioning stages, along with a microcontroller-based interface for real-time control. Emphasis is placed on achieving high frequency stability, low total harmonic distortion, and user-friendly operation.

The device aims to bridge the gap between affordability and functionality, addressing the needs of users in resource-constrained settings. This paper details the design methodology, technological innovations, and the potential impact of this versatile function generator on modern testing and prototyping environments.

7 Motivation

Development of a Cost-Effective and Versatile Function Generator

The motivation for development of a function generator from scratch is driven by the need for a cost-effective, precise, and versatile signal generation solution tailored for academic, research, and small-scale industrial applications. Existing commercial function generators, while highly capable, often come with significant financial and operational constraints, limiting accessibility for resource-constrained environments.

Our team identified this gap through a comprehensive evaluation of current tools and their limitations. This project offers an opportunity to design a device that not only fulfills the essential requirements of waveform generation—producing sinusoidal, square, and triangular signals—but also ensures high frequency stability, low harmonic distortion, and user-friendly operation.

The project embodies our commitment to advancing practical engineering skills while addressing real-world challenges. By integrating principles of circuit design, signal processing, and digital control, the function generator aims to bridge the affordability gap without compromising on performance. This initiative is not merely an academic exercise but a step toward democratizing access to essential testing equipment, thereby empowering the broader engineering and scientific community.

8 Requirements

8.1 Input Requirements

  • Waveform Control:

    • System must accept digital inputs for waveform selection (Sine, Square, Triangle, Ramp, and Pulse waves)

  • Frequency Range:

    • User input for frequency selection from 1Hz to 10MHz through rotary encoder interface

  • Amplitude Control:

    • Variable amplitude control from 0V to 5V peak-to-peak through user interface

  • Phase Control:

    • 0-360 degrees phase adjustment capability when needed

  • Power Input:

    • Since, the client hasn’t specified the power requirements. We assume the power supply must be standard power supply, which can be usually available at laboratory spaces

  • User Interface:

    • Rotary encoder with push-button functionality for parameter selection and adjustment, and buttons for user selection

  • Digital Control:

    • SPI interface between ESP32 and AD9833 for waveform generation

8.2 Output Requirements

  • Waveform Quality:

    • Clean output waveforms with minimal distortion (THD < 1% for sine wave)

  • Frequency Accuracy:

    • Generated frequency must be as close to the desired value as possible

  • Amplitude Stability:

    • Output amplitude must remain stable as close as possible to the desired value

  • Display Output:

    • Real-time display of frequency, waveform type, and amplitude settings on OLED/LCD

  • Signal Output:

    • Industry-standard BNC connector with 50 output impedance

  • Noise Level:

    • Output signal-to-noise ratio to be kept as high as possible

8.3 Power Requirements

  • Input Power Source:

    • USB Type-C power delivery
    • Standard mobile charger (5V)
    • Power rating: 15W-20W capability recommended

  • Protection:

    • Over-current protection for >2.5A
    • Reverse polarity protection
    • Short circuit protection
    • Thermal protection

  • Power Supply Requirements:

    • USB Type-C charger: 5V/3A or better
    • Good quality power supply recommended

8.4 Environmental Requirements

  • Operating Conditions:

    • Temperature: +0°C to +40°C.
    • Humidity: Up to 80% relative humidity (non-condensing).

  • Storage Conditions:

    • Temperature: -10°C to +70°C.
    • Humidity: Up to 70% relative humidity.

8.5 Site (Usage Site) Requirements

  • Location:

    • Designed for indoor use in laboratory or workshop environments.

  • Space Requirements:

    • Compact design to fit on standard lab benches (dimensions: 20cm x 15cm x 5cm maximum).

  • Power Supply:

    • Standard 5V power source availability.

  • Ventilation:

    • Adequate airflow around the device to prevent overheating.

  • Lighting:

    • Sufficient lighting for display readability.

8.6 Structural Requirements

  • Dimensions:

    • Maximum size of 200mm × 150mm × 100mm.

  • Material:

    • Enclosure: 2mm aluminium sheets for durability and heat dissipation or some enclosure which provides shielding.
    • Front Panel: 2mm acrylic sheet for labeling and controls.

  • Components:

    • Rotary encoders, rotary potentiometer, BNC connectors, push buttons, square push button.

  • Assembly:

    • Modular design for easy assembly and disassembly.
    • Rubber stoppers for stability and vibration damping.
    • Bolts and nuts for secure fastening.

  • Slidable Top Lid:

    • For easy access to internal components during maintenance.

  • Access Points:

    • Easy access to calibration and test points.

8.7 Time Requirements

  • Design Time Requirement:

    • Total design time: 4 weeks
    • Week 1: Ideation and concept development

    • Weeks 2-4: Design implementation and testing

      • Week 1: Simulations and parameter identification
      • Week 2: Basic assembly and initial testing
      • Week 3: Detailed testing and refinement

  • Time to Market Requirement:

    • Prototype completion: 4 weeks from project initiation
    • First unit delivery: End of week 4
    • Total time to market: 4 weeks

  • Lifetime Requirement:

    • Minimum operational lifetime: 2 years
    • Maintenance interval: Every 6 months

    • Component lifetime specifications:

      • Electronic components: Minimum 2 years
      • Mechanical components: 10,000 operations
      • Display: 10,000 hours

    • Performance degradation limits:

      • Frequency drift: ±1% per year
      • Amplitude drift: ±2% per year

  • End of Life Requirement:

    • Disposal requirements:

      • Electronic components: Separate for e-waste disposal
      • Material segregation: Separate plastic and metal components

    • Recycling specifications:

      • Metal case: 100% recyclable
      • PCB: E-waste recycling protocol
      • Components: Material-type separation required

    • Post-production support:

      • Spare parts availability: 1 year after end-of-life
      • Support timeline: 3 years after end-of-life
